Projects

Somascan: Fatigue in Sjogren’s syndrome

Primary Sjögren’s syndrome (pSS) is a systemic autoimmune disease, where patients often suffer from fatigue. Biological pathways underlying this fatigue are unknown. In this study aptamer-based SOMAscan technology is used to identify biomarkers and treatment targets for fatigue in pSS.

miRNAs: TPLL

T-cell prolymphocytic leukemia (T-PLL) is a rare neoplasm with an adverse prognosis. Moreover accurate and fast prognosis is problematic due to the high heterogeneity of this disease. Therefore, further molecular insights and deep understanding is needed.

Transcriptomics: Exhausted Linf T

Chronic infections often results in T cell exhaustion. To determine the molecular signature of exhaustion, we compared cell exposed repetitively to a peptide with those ones which were not exposed or exposed one time, to compare the transcriptomic profile of the exhausted cells.

WES and transcriptomics: NeoAntigenes

Cancer immunology is evolving as the new mainstream approach to fight tumor progression. The use of immunotherapeutic agents mediating immune checkpoint receptor blockage has demonstrate a better anti-tumor efficacy compared to conventional therapies. Syngenic mouse cancer models are particularly for preclinical drug testing.
